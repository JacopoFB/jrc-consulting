About

Empowering families and educators to help every student thrive

Joo Ree Chu is a School Psychologist with a wide breadth of professional experience. For over a decade she has worked in both public and private school settings to support children, their caregivers, teachers, and school leadership. She believes every parent is the expert of their child and wants the best for them. When it comes to educational matters, Joo Ree is committed to partnering with families to guide and support them through uncertainty and setbacks. Her work is driven by the principle that supportive and equitable learning environments result in better outcomes for all.

Joo Ree is the proud mother of two children who have attended both public and private schools. She understands the trials and tribulations of parenthood and what it means to take life one day (sometimes one hour!) at a time. She is a lifelong dancer, swimmer, and enjoys finding respite in nature. Joo Ree also enjoys traveling abroad to learn about different cultures and educational systems to broaden her professional perspective. JRC Partnerships is honored to redistribute 10% of all service fees to community-serving organizations in the U.S. and overseas.

Services

  • Advocating for specialized school supports ▸
    Assistance with understanding and advocating for your child's educational strengths and areas of growth, as well as related specialized support services. This may include attending and pre/post meeting strategizing for school meetings related to accommodation plans (504), Individualized Education Programs (IEP), initial or triennial special education evaluations, and gifted services.
  • Review and analysis of evaluations/interventions ▸
    Comprehensive review and analysis of:

    A. School records, standardized test scores, and supports provided within the general and/or special education settings

    B. External evaluations, specialized assessments, and support services
  • Navigating school and/or district decisions and grievance procedures related to student support services ▸
    Support with understanding, advocating for, and contesting matters within Special Education, Gifted Services, and ADA accommodations (e.g., evaluation request, grade retention or advancement, special circumstances).
  • Direct observation and accommodation recommendations ▸
    Direct observation in school and community settings plus recommendations for accommodations and tools (e.g., executive functioning, adaptive skills, routines).
  • Referral to external evaluation and support providers ▸
    Referrals to evaluation clinics and support service providers (e.g., psycho-educational evaluations, social-emotional/behavioral therapy, speech services, occupational therapy, sensory processing therapy, academic tutoring).
  • Coordination of support providers across settings ▸
    Support with coordinating and communicating with service providers across settings (e.g., school, home, clinic, and court-ordered supports).
  • School recommendations and admission assistance ▸
    Recommendations for public and independent (private) schools, as well as support related to admission procedures (e.g., attending campus visits; advocating for child's unique strengths, areas of growth, and related supports needed; managing application submission).
  • Cognitive testing ▸
    Standalone cognitive testing as needed for private school admission applications.
